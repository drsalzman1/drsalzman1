Double Deck Pinochle - Rules

You play south. Your partner plays north. Your opponents play west and east. The deck has 4 suits of 20 cards: 4 aces, tens, kings, queens and jacks in that order.

The dealer, initially chosen at random, shuffles and deals the entire deck. The player clockwise from the dealer must bid at least 50 or pass. Subsequent bidders must bid higher (5 higher starting at 60) or pass. Bidding continues until only one bidder remains. If the first three bidders pass, the fourth bidder must bid at least 50. The final bidder picks the trump suit, which beats other suits, and plays first.

The first player may lead any card. The next player clockwise must follow the lead suit if possible, and if the lead's not been trumped, must beat the high card in the lead suit if possible. If they can't follow the lead, they must play trump if possible, and beat any high trump if possible. If they can't follow the lead or play trump, they can play any card. Play continues until everyone's played a card.

The winner then collects the trick, and if any cards remain in their hand, leads the next trick. When the hand is over, the player clockwise from dealer deals the next hand until the game is over.
Double Deck Pinochle - Scoring

Each player's meld comes from their hand:

  • A (ace) in each suit (around):10; double:100
  • K (king) in each suit:8; double:80
  • Q (queen) in each suit:6; double:60
  • J (jack) in each suit:4; double:40
  • A, 10, K, Q, J (run) in trump:16; double:150
  • Q of spades, J of diamonds:4; 2x:30; 3x:90
  • K, Q in trump (not in run):4; double:8
  • K, Q (marriage) in non-trump:2; double:4

A team's meld is the sum of each member's meld. If a team's meld is less than 20, their meld is 0.

After bidding, each player reveals their meld. Even if a player's team melds less than 20, the player must reveal if they have aces around. If a player wins the bid without a run or marraige in trump, they can't play (must toss) the hand. The bid winner may also choose to toss a hand.

After each hand, each team counts their take. Each ace, ten and king is worth 1. Taking the last trick is worth 2. If a team's take is less than 20, their take is 0.

Normally, each team's score increases by their meld plus take. However, if the bid-winning team's meld plus take is less than their bid, their score decreases by their bid. The first team to reach 500, wins. If both teams reach 500, the bid-winning team wins.
