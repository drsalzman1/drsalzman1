Set Up

To start a game:

  • Select your name to identify yourself
  • Select bot/human (/) to identify type
  • Select bot names, if any, to identify bots
  • Announce the game number to inform humans
  • Check/uncheck options to show assistance
  • Select joining human names to identify humans
  • Click Start to deal the cards

To join a game:

  • Select your name to identify yourself
  • Type game number to pick which game
  • Click Join to join the game
  • Wait for the cards to be dealt

The player who starts the game will shuffle and deal the deck.

Terms
Bidding

The player to the dealer's left must bid at least 50 or pass. The next player must bid higher (5 higher starting at 60) or pass. This continues until someone wins the bid. If three players pass, the fourth must bid. The bid winner picks trump and decides whether to play.

If you win the bid, you'll pick trump and play the first card. However, if you don't have a marriage in trump or your team's meld plus take is less than your bid, your team's score will be reduced by your bid.

Since normally your partner will have at least 10 meld and your team will take at least 20 of the 50 points, it's usually safe to bid your meld plus 30 points. Of course, if you have a strong hand or your partner has lots of meld, you can bid higher.

Starting the bid at 52 or jumping the bid by 2 tells your partner that you have a marriage and about 20 meld. Likewise, 53/3 implies 30, 54/4 implies 40, etc.

If your team's meld is at least 20, you reveal your meld when bidding is complete. Otherwise, you only reveal aces around.

Terms
Playing

If you win the bid without a marriage in trump, you must toss the hand. Otherwise, you can choose to lead any card or toss the hand.

The player to the left must follow the lead suit if possible, and if the lead's not been trumped, must beat the high card in the lead suit if possible. If they can't follow the lead, they must play trump if possible, and beat any high trump if possible. If they can't follow the lead or play trump, they can play any card. Play continues until everyone's played a card.

If you played the highest card, you collect the trick and lead any card. When everyone's played all of their cards, the player to the left of dealer deals the next hand. The game is over when some team's score reaches 500.

Terms
Scoring

After each hand, each team counts their take. Each ace, ten and king is worth 1 point. Taking the last trick is worth an additional 2 points. If a team's take is less than 20, it won't count towards their score. Likewise, if a team's meld is less than 20, it won't count towards their score.

Normally, each team's score increases by their meld plus take. However, if the bid-winning team's meld plus take is less than their bid, their score decreases by their bid. The first team to reach 500, wins. If both teams reach 500, the bid-winning team wins.

Terms
Communicating

You can't directly communicate your cards or strategy to your partner. This would be talking across the table. However, you can communicate certain information by the way you bid and play.

For example, if you start the bid at 52 or increase the bid by 2, your partner may assume you have a marriage and 20 meld. Likewise, 53/3 implies 30, 54/4 implies 40, etc.

If you play an ace or jack on your partner's ace, your partner may be encouraged to lead that suit when it's convenient.

If you start the game, you can choose to show all hands. This allows everyone to cheat learn.

Terms
Counts

You know your own cards. You reveal your cards to others when you lay down your meld, play a card, trump the lead suit, don't beat a high card, or don't follow the lead suit.

If you start the game, you can choose to show card counts, including:

  • Outstanding trump (trump in the other players' hands) next to the trump suit ()
  • Outstanding aces and cards in each suit when the trump suit is clicked
  • Colored suits ( ) next to the other players' names:
    • White - player has an ace in that suit
    • Light gray - player may have an ace in suit
    • Dark gray - player has no ace in that suit
    • Black - player has no cards in that suit
Terms
Strategy

You should bid up to a limit based on your meld, your estimate of your partner's meld (perhaps based on a jump bid), and your estimate of the strength of your playing hand.

You should count the cards that players reveal as meld. For example, if someone reveals a king of clubs but no queen of clubs, you know they don't have a queen of clubs. You should also count the cards players reveal during play. For example, if someone plays a jack of clubs on a king of clubs, you know they don't have a ten or ace of clubs.

If a bot estimates that its team can win the trick, it plays the card that it estimates will take the trick, take the most points, spend the fewest of its team's high and trump cards, and waste the most of its opponents' high and trump cards. If the bot estimates that its team will lose the trick, it plays the card that it estimates will lose the fewest points, waste the fewest of its team's high and trump cards, and waste the most of its opponents' high and trump cards.

Terms
Terms
  • Players: the four named players
  • Teams: you and your partner vs. them
  • Suit: spades, hearts, clubs, diamonds
  • Rank: ace, ten, king, queen, jack (in that order)
  • Deck: 80 cards - 4 of each rank in each suit
  • Hand: cards held by a player - initially 20
  • Bid: compete for right to pick trump and lead
  • Pass: withdraw from bidding
  • Trump: the suit that outranks the other suits
  • Lead: the first card played
  • Trick: a set of four played cards
  • Meld: valuable sets of cards in a player's hand
  • Around: one specific rank in each suit
  • Marriage: king and queen in a suit
  • Run: one of each rank in a suit
  • Pinochle: queen of spades & jack of diamonds
  • Take: points earned by the trick winner
  • Toss: decide to not play a hand

Your team's meld is the sum of your meld and your partner's meld. If your team's meld for a hand is less than 20, it won't count towards your team's score.

Terms
Take

Points your team earns by winning tricks.

Aces, tens and kings are each worth one point. Queens and jacks are worth zero points.

The player with the highest card wins the trick. An ace beats a ten. A ten beats a king. A king beats a queen. A queen beats a jack. Any trump beats a non-trump.

Winning the last trick in a hand earns an extra two points.

Your team's take is the sum of your take and your partner's take. If your team's take for a hand is less than 20, it won't count towards your team's score.
