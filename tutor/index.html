Sudoku Tutor
This app teaches you to solve a Sudoku puzzle using the Nishio (trial and error) method. To start, pencil in the squares from a puzzle by touching squares and entering numbers. To erase a mistake, change the mode to erase squares, touch the square, and change the mode back to pencil in squares. When done, change the mode to pencil in marks.
Next, pencil in the obvious marks by touching squares and entering numbers. For example, a 1 and 2 in the top-left square indicates that the top-left square may be a 1 or a 2. That is, neither a 1 nor a 2 have been penciled into the top row, left row, or the top-left 3x3 box of squares. To erase a mistake, change the mode to erase marks, touch the square, enter the number, and change the mode back to pencil in marks. When done, change the mode to pencil in squares.
Next, pencil in each square with only one mark and erase the corresponding marks in that square's row, column and box. If the puzzle isn't yet solved, tentatively pencil in a square with only two marks and then continue solving the puzzle until it is either solved or you hit a dead end. The tutor will number each tentative square and save the puzzle. If you hit a dead end, erase the most recent tentative square to recall the puzzle at that point.
